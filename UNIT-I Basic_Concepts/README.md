# Unit 1 – Basic Concepts (Digital System Design)

## About This Unit

Unit 1 is the foundation of Digital System Design.  
It focuses on the fundamental concepts that are essential for understanding how digital systems represent, process, and manipulate information.

This unit builds the base for everything that comes later, including combinational circuits, sequential circuits, and Verilog HDL implementation.

---

## What I Am Learning in This Unit

In this unit, I am focusing on understanding concepts deeply rather than memorizing them. My goal is to connect theory with practical logic design.

### Topics Covered:

- Number Systems (Decimal, Binary, Octal, Hexadecimal)
- Number System Conversions
- Complements (1’s and 2’s Complement)
- Binary Codes (BCD, Gray Code, Excess-3)
- Digital Logic Gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR)
- Boolean Algebra (Laws, Theorems, Postulates)
- Boolean Functions (SOP, POS, Canonical Forms)
- Simplification Techniques (K-Map, Boolean Method, Tabulation)
- Universal Gates (NAND and NOR implementations)

---

## My Learning Approach

For every topic in this unit, I follow a structured learning process:

- Understand the theory behind the concept  
- Analyze truth tables and logic behavior  
- Solve manual examples  
- Visualize circuit diagrams  
- Connect logic to real-world digital systems  
- Prepare interview questions for revision  

I only consider a topic complete when I can explain it clearly without referring to notes.

---

## Why This Unit Is Important

Unit 1 is the backbone of Digital Electronics. It helps in:

- Building strong logic design thinking  
- Understanding how data is represented in hardware  
- Simplifying complex digital expressions  
- Preparing for Verilog HDL and RTL design  
- Solving interview-level digital design problems  

Without mastering this unit, advanced topics like FSMs, ALUs, and processors become difficult to understand.

## Outcome After Completing This Unit

By the end of Unit 1, I will be able to:

- Confidently convert between number systems  
- Simplify Boolean expressions efficiently  
- Understand and design logic gates  
- Apply Boolean algebra in circuit design  
- Solve basic digital logic problems independently
